Fun Facts

World's First Computer Programmer: Ada Lovelace, an English mathematician, is often regarded as the world's first computer programmer. She wrote an algorithm for Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine in the mid-1800s. The First Computer Bug:The term "bug" in the context of computer problems originated in 1947 when Grace Hopper, a computer scientist, found a moth inside a computer relay. She taped it into her logbook, noting it as the "first actual case of a bug." The Largest Computer:The Tianhe-2, a supercomputer developed in China, was one of the world's most powerful computers. It could perform over 33 quadrillion calculations per second (33 petaflops) as of my last update in 2022.
